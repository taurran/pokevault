# Your Knowledge Vault

Welcome. This is your **Second Brain + LLM Wiki** — a portable, Obsidian-native vault where
raw material you collect gets compiled by an AI assistant into persistent, interlinked notes
that compound over time.

**Open this folder (`~/PokeVault/`) as an Obsidian vault.**

## How it works (30 seconds)

1. **Drop** anything worth remembering into a zone's `wiki/raw/inbox/`.
2. **Ask your AI assistant** (Claude, Cursor, a local LLM, or a desktop AI app) to "process my
   inbox" — it reads `AGENTS.md` and compiles your sources into wiki pages.
3. **Query** the wiki instead of re-reading the pile. Knowledge accumulates; it never resets.

## The four zones

| Zone | What goes here | Privacy |
|---|---|---|
| **`second-brain/`** | Who you are (`profile/`) + everything you personally know | Private |
| **`work/`** | Project/engagement notes, deliverables, work knowledge | Private |
| **`personal/`** | Your life: people (CRM), areas, goals, calendar/key-dates | Private |
| **`toolkit/`** | Your own AI skills, agents, and context files | Private |

One shared **`daily/`** note (work + personal) is your capture surface — synthesis routes each piece
to the right zone. Plus two non-knowledge folders at the root (kept out of Obsidian's search and
graph): **`projects/`** (code workspaces) and **`scratch/`** (transient staging).

## The rules that keep it trustworthy

Your assistant follows seven **anti-forgetting rules** (full text in `AGENTS.md` §4). The short
version: it appends instead of overwriting, never invents without a source, flags contradictions
instead of hiding them, and treats your raw files as read-only. Your knowledge is safe.
